Saturday Internship Projects

Internship Project - Summer 2024

Project Overview

During my internship at Saturday Robotics, I was tasked with designing and manufacturing a centralized control system for pneumatic operations. The goal was to create a professional, ergonomic housing for a series of regulators and switches that prioritized accessible user input and organized internal tubing management.

Key Features

  • Ergonomic Layout: Designed for comfortable, high-frequency usage with intuitive switch placement.
  • Integrated Tubing Management: Internal routing paths designed to prevent kinks and maintain a clean, serviceable workspace.
  • Modular Frame: Built using 80/20 aluminum extrusion for easy mounting and future hardware expansions.

Technical Implementation

  • CAD Modeling: Utilized SolidWorks to iterate through potential layouts, ensuring all pneumatic components had sufficient clearance for both the fittings and the minimum bend radius of the tubing.
  • Rapid Prototyping: Transitioned from digital models to physical assembly by laser cutting custom mounting plates from stock material in the machine shop.
  • Structural Design: Engineered a supporting chassis using 80/20 stock metal, ensuring the panel remained rigid under the mechanical load of the switches and regulators.
  • Manufacturing: Managed the full lifecycle from brainstorming and material selection to final assembly and pneumatic testing.

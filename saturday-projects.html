Mechanical design and manufacturing, Summer 2024

Saturday Robotics Internship

SolidWorks DFM Laser cutting Pneumatics

During my internship at Saturday Robotics, I took ownership of several hardware projects spanning fluid control systems, sheet metal adapters, and custom tooling.

Project 1

Pneumatic control panel

I designed and manufactured a centralized control system for pneumatic operations. The goal was to create a professional, ergonomic housing for regulators and switches while keeping the internal tubing organized and serviceable.

Ergonomic layout

Designed for comfortable, high-frequency use with intuitive switch placement and readable control grouping.

Integrated tubing management

Created internal routing paths to prevent kinks, protect fittings, and maintain a clean operating workspace.

Modular frame

Used 80/20 aluminum extrusion to support future hardware changes and straightforward mounting.

Project 2

Universal sheet metal adapter plates

I designed a versatile sheet metal adapter plate capable of mounting an electrical control housing box to over 70 unique locations on the system chassis.

This required extensive concept generation to ensure universal compatibility and secure mounting from a single standardized design. I used SolidWorks for 3D modeling, then moved through rapid prototyping and final manufacturing.

Project 3

High-tolerance custom wrench

To service a specialized component with specific clearance requirements, I engineered a custom hand tool. Standard wrenches could not interface safely with the part, so a bespoke tool was needed.

The wrench was modeled in CAD to verify precise geometric fit, then manufactured by laser-cutting a custom headplate and welding it to a solid steel rod handle. The final assembly was held to tight tolerances to prevent stripping or damage during installation and maintenance.

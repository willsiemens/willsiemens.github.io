Saturday Robotics Internship

Mechanical Design & Manufacturing - Summer 2024

During my internship at Saturday Robotics, I took ownership of several hardware projects spanning from fluid control systems to custom tooling. I managed the full lifecycle of these components, from initial concept and CAD modeling to in-house manufacturing.

1. Pneumatic Control Panel

I was tasked with designing and manufacturing a centralized control system for pneumatic operations. The goal was to create a professional, ergonomic housing for a series of regulators and switches that prioritized accessible user input and organized internal tubing management.

Key Features

  • Ergonomic Layout: Designed for comfortable, high-frequency usage with intuitive switch placement.
  • Integrated Tubing Management: Internal routing paths designed to prevent kinks and maintain a clean workspace.
  • Modular Frame: Built using 80/20 aluminum extrusion for easy mounting and hardware expansions.

Technical Implementation

  • CAD Modeling: Utilized SolidWorks to ensure all components had sufficient clearance for fittings and minimum bend radii.
  • Manufacturing: Transitioned from digital models to physical assembly by laser cutting custom mounting plates from stock material.
  • Structural Design: Engineered a supporting chassis to ensure rigidity under the mechanical load of the switches.

2. Universal Sheet Metal Adapter Plates

I was tasked with designing a versatile sheet metal adapter plate capable of mounting an electrical control housing box to over 70 unique locations on the system chassis.

This required extensive concept generation to ensure universal compatibility and secure mounting from a single standardized plate design. I navigated the full Design for Manufacturing (DFM) pipeline, utilizing SolidWorks for the 3D modeling, followed by rapid prototyping and final manufacturing.

Adapter plate design and manufacturing

3. High-Tolerance Custom Wrench

Laser-cut and welded custom tooling

To service a specialized component with highly specific clearance requirements, I engineered a customized hand tool. Standard wrenches could not interface safely with the part, necessitating a bespoke solution.

The wrench was first modeled in CAD to verify precise geometric fit. I then manufactured the tool by laser-cutting a custom headplate and welding it directly to a solid steel rod handle. The final assembly was held to tight tolerances to guarantee a perfect fit, preventing any stripping or damage to the custom part during routine installation and maintenance.

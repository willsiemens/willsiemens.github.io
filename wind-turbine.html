Wind Turbine Design & Prototyping

CAD Class Project - Fall 2022

For this freshman engineering project, I collaborated with a small team to design, prototype, and test a 3D-printed model wind turbine. The project encompassed the full engineering design cycle: initial brainstorming, 3D modeling, rapid prototyping, and empirical testing of both the blade's power output and the tower's structural integrity.

Leadership & Core Contributions

  • Blade Design & Manufacturing: Led the aerodynamic design and 3D printing of the turbine blades, researching optimal profiles to maximize power generation.
  • Structural CAD: Designed and 3D printed the bottom section of the turbine base.
  • Project Management: Acted as team facilitator by assigning workloads, managing deadlines, and ensuring equitable distribution of tasks across the group.

Engineering Constraints & Challenges

  • Strict Material Constraints: The tower base was required to reach a height of 16 inches using a maximum of only 17 cubic inches of ABS plastic.
  • Manufacturing Limitations: Due to printer volume constraints, the base had to be designed and printed in two separate 8-inch assemblies.
  • Knowledge Gap: Overcame an initial lack of knowledge regarding wind turbine physics through independent research and iterative design.

Results & Future Iterations

The final blade design performed exceptionally well, yielding high power output during physical testing. However, Finite Element Analysis (FEA) and applied horizontal load testing revealed that our tower structure was lacking support in its upper half.

For future iterations, I would redesign the tower with a thicker, more compact truss structure to better withstand applied wind forces and bending moments. Overall, this project served as a highly engaging and foundational introduction to CAD, FEA, and the iterative design process.

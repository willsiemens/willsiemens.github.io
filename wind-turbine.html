CAD class project, Fall 2022

Wind Turbine Design and Prototyping

CAD FEA 3D printing Testing

A model wind turbine project covering the full engineering design cycle from brainstorming and 3D modeling to rapid prototyping and empirical testing.

Project overview

For this freshman engineering project, I collaborated with a small team to design, prototype, and test a 3D-printed model wind turbine. We evaluated both blade power output and tower structural integrity.

The project served as a hands-on introduction to CAD, FEA, design constraints, manufacturing limitations, and iterative physical testing.

Prototype testing setup.

Blade side-profile CAD work.

Blade design and manufacturing

Led aerodynamic blade design and 3D printing, researching profiles that could maximize power generation during testing.

Structural CAD

Designed and printed the bottom section of the turbine base while working within strict material and height constraints.

Project management

Acted as team facilitator by assigning workloads, managing deadlines, and supporting an equitable distribution of tasks.

Constraints and challenges

  • The tower base needed to reach 16 inches using a maximum of 17 cubic inches of ABS plastic.
  • Printer volume constraints required the base to be designed as two separate 8-inch assemblies.
  • The team overcame an initial knowledge gap in wind turbine physics through research and iteration.

FEA showing stress concentration in the upper tower half.

Results

Testing drove the next design iteration.

The final blade design performed well and produced high power output during physical testing. However, FEA and applied horizontal load testing showed that the tower structure lacked support in the upper half.

For a future iteration, I would redesign the tower with a thicker, more compact truss structure to better withstand wind forces and bending moments.
